# Assessment

Cohorts are pass/fail. Most grading is mechanical and peer-driven; staff's narrow job is determining suitability for job offers — which the market largely decides through hiring partners reviewing public work.

## Components

- [metrics.md](metrics.md) — the measurable signals per project
- [peer-review-system.md](peer-review-system.md) — how peer review and rating works mechanically
- [pass-fail.md](pass-fail.md) — what passing a cohort requires
- [job-offer-readiness.md](job-offer-readiness.md) — the staff determination and its criteria

## What "suitable for a job offer" means (decided)

The student has demonstrated, repeatedly and verifiably:

1. They can **review others' work** well (peer review record, PR triage if they operated a platform)
2. They can **produce work others approve** (winner votes, user adoption, investor interest, merged PRs)
3. They can **contribute at a high level** to codebases they don't own (PRs against cohort platforms, merged open source PRs)

All three leave a public GitHub trail, so hiring partners can verify them independently.
