# Guarantee, refunds, retakes, buyouts

**Purpose:** Define the exact commercial terms of the program promise — what students pay, what they get, how they exit, and what triggers the job-offer guarantee. These terms populate the Program Agreement signed at enrollment.

---

## The guarantee (student-facing summary)

> Pay **$10,000 once**. Attend as many cohorts as you need. Complete the program successfully and receive a **qualifying job offer**, or continue re-enrolling until you do. Withdraw in week 1 for a full refund.

---

## Qualifying job offer

A job offer **fulfills the guarantee** and **closes the student's enrollment obligation** (no further re-enrollment rights, though alumni services may continue informally).

### Qualifies

| Criterion | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| Role type | Full-time employment, primary duties in **software development, engineering, DevOps, or closely related technical IC or tech-lead role** |
| Compensation | **≥ $80,000 USD** base salary (or local equivalent at enrollment-time FX rate published by program) |
| Duration | Permanent or **≥ 12-month contract** |
| Start date | Within **180 days** of the student's most recent cohort pass date |
| Verification | Written offer letter or employer confirmation accepted by program placement staff |
| Source | **Any source** — hiring partner, self-sourced, inbound. Referral fee owed only when hire is through a signed partner agreement. |

### Does not qualify

| Situation | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Internship, unpaid, or stipend-only role | Does not fulfill guarantee; student may re-enroll |
| Part-time (< 32 hrs/week) | Does not fulfill |
| Founder's own startup with no salary | Does not fulfill until company pays ≥ $80k salary |
| Offer withdrawn before start | Does not fulfill; student re-enrolls |
| Student declines all offers that meet criteria | Guarantee remains open; staff documents declined offers |

**Reasoning:** The $80k floor and full-time requirement filter out symbolic offers while staying realistic for junior-to-mid agent-fluent developers in major markets. Self-sourced offers count because the guarantee is about employability, not partner revenue.

---

## Student obligations (voiding the guarantee)

The guarantee is **void** (student may still re-enroll, but program has no obligation to actively place) if the student:

1. **Misses > 20% of mandatory checkpoints** in a cohort term (kickoffs, review submissions, vote participation) without approved accommodation.
2. **Fails to submit all six project units** in a term without approved extension.
3. **Engages in harassment, plagiarism, or deliberate sabotage** of cohort platforms (documented, program director determination).
4. **Refuses to participate in placement process** for 60 consecutive days after receiving a Pass determination (ignores partner intros, skips showcase, no communication).

Accommodations (medical, visa, family) pause obligation clocks; program director approves in writing.

## Refund terms

| Event | Amount | Timeline |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Withdraw before cohort start | 100% ($10,000) | Processed within 14 business days |
| Withdraw during week 1 (by Friday 5pm cohort-local) | 100% ($10,000) | Processed within 14 business days |
| Withdraw after week 1 | $0 cash refund | Student retains re-enrollment rights |
| Program cancels cohort before start | 100% or transfer to next cohort (student choice) | 14 business days |
| Program-initiated buyout | $5,000 | Within 14 business days of signed release |

Tooling subscriptions (Cursor, Claude Code) are not refunded by the program.

**Reasoning:** Week-1 full refund honors "quit anytime" for genuine misfits. After week 1, the student's economic remedy is free re-enrollment — which costs the program near-zero — not cash extraction mid-cohort.

## Repeat enrollment (retakes)

| Term | Policy |
|------|--------|
| Cost | **$0** — covered by original $10,000 payment |
| Eligibility | Any enrolled student: failed cohort, passed but wants more reps, platform winner wanting lighter build load next term |
| Limit | **Unlimited** until qualifying job offer |
| Registration | Term registration form each cohort; priority after new admits if cohort full |
| Tooling | Student pays $400/month subscriptions each term attended |

No retake fee. Repeat enrollment is a normal path, not remedial.

---

## Buyout policy

When a student is a poor fit — disruptive, repeatedly failing obligations, or creating cohort-wide harm — the program may offer a **buyout** instead of carrying them through another term.

| Field | Policy |
|-------|--------|
| Amount | **$5,000** (default; program director may request up to $7,500 with EVP approval for high-risk cases) |
| Trigger | Program-initiated only; not a standing student option |
| Approval | Program director recommends → EVP or delegate approves |
| Student receives | $5,000 + written release |
| Program receives | Signed **Release and Separation Agreement**: mutual release, confidentiality, non-disparagement (mutual), forfeiture of re-enrollment and guarantee rights |
| Timing | Offer valid 7 days; payment within 14 business days of signed release |
| Confidentiality | Buyout existence and terms confidential unless student waives |

**Reasoning:** $5k is half tuition — cheaper than a disruptive student consuming 29 peer reviews × 3 projects, poisoning cohort culture, and generating placement liability. Case-by-case (not standing offer) prevents gaming.

## Liability modeling

### Worst-case scenarios (cohort of 30, cohort 1)

| Scenario | Exposure | Mitigation |
|----------|----------|------------|
| 100% week-1 refund | $300,000 | Stagger admissions confirmation; charge at week 1 start not application |
| 30 pass, 0 placements in 12 months | $0 cash (guarantee = continued enrollment, not cash payout) + staff placement cost | Build partner pipeline before week 16; showcase is contractual partner touchpoint |
| 10 buyouts in one cohort | $50,000 | Strict admissions filter + week-1 exit; buyout is last resort |
| 30 pass, all re-enroll 3× | $0 incremental tuition; ~$3,600/student tooling only (student-paid) | Near-zero marginal cost assumption holds |
| Referral fee clawback after kickback paid | Net loss = 90% of fee retained if clawed back within clawback window | Clawback passed through per placement agreement |

### Reserve recommendation (cohort 1)

| Reserve | Amount | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Refund reserve | **$150,000** | 50% of max cohort tuition if entire cohort exits week 1 |
| Buyout reserve | **$50,000** | 10 buyouts |
| Placement ops | **$75,000/year** | 1 FTE placement lead (see [financial-model.md](financial-model.md)) |

## Program Agreement structure

The Program Agreement (legal template — not written here) must contain:

1. Tuition: $10,000 one-time, what it covers
2. Guarantee definition: qualifying job offer (table above)
3. Student obligations and void conditions
4. Refund schedule
5. Repeat enrollment terms
6. Buyout terms
7. Referral kickback (10% of fee collected — see [placement-referrals.md](placement-referrals.md))
8. IP and public work consent (pointer to [institutional/legal-risk.md](../institutional/legal-risk.md))
9. Dispute resolution: **binding arbitration**, program campus jurisdiction
