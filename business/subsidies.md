# Subsidies

**Purpose:** When and how to subsidize tooling, tuition, and living costs once revenue-positive.

---

## Trigger

| Milestone | Unlocks |
|-----------|---------|
| **Cumulative margin ≥ $500k** OR **cohort 2 start** | Tooling subsidies |
| **Cumulative margin ≥ $1.2M** | Need-based tuition assistance |
| **Cumulative margin ≥ $2M** + EVP approval | Housing/food pilot |

Cohort 1: **no subsidies** except ⚠️ EVP one-off hardship grants.

---

## Priority order

1. **Tooling** ($400/mo × 4 × student) — universal need, easy to administer
2. **Need-based tuition** — widens access
3. **Housing/food** — Gauntlet-style; highest cost, pilot only

---

## Tooling subsidy (cohort 2+)

| Eligibility | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Demonstrated financial need (application + W-2/FAFSA equivalent) | 100% Cursor + Claude for term |
| Hult student scholarship lane (if approved) | 100% tooling |
| Platform winner returning | 50% tooling (merit) |

Cap: **10 seats per cohort** need-based; unlimited merit at 50% for winners.

Administered: student pays → reimbursed within 30 days of term start.

---

## Tuition assistance

| Level | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| Partial | $2,500 off (Hult student lane if EVP approves) |
| Full | Case-by-case; ⚠️ EVP only |

Does not stack with repeat enrollment (already paid).

---

## Housing/food pilot

10 students, 1 campus, 1 term. Estimated $3k/student/term. Requires dedicated ops hire — not cohort 2.

---
