# Hult policy review

**Purpose:** Determine how the Hult Cohort Program fits inside Hult International University's enrollment, credit, refund, and accreditation rules — and what must change before cohort 1 can launch.

This document recommends a **non-credit continuing-education certificate program** administered through Hult, with a separate enrollment agreement for the job guarantee and placement terms. That structure preserves the program's distinctive economics (one-time fee, unlimited repeat enrollment, pass/fail, money-back guarantee) while keeping the university's degree programs insulated.

---

## Recommended enrollment structure

### Enrollment status

**Recommendation:** Cohort students enroll as **Hult continuing-education (non-degree) students** in a named certificate program: *Hult Cohort Developer Program*.

| Option | Verdict | Reasoning |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Degree-seeking Hult student | Not recommended | Credit-hour rules, grading scales, and accreditation review cycles conflict with pass/fail, peer-driven assessment, and the job guarantee. |
| Separate legal entity only | Not recommended | Complicates Hult brand, campus access, and international student visa sponsorship. |
| **Continuing-ed certificate under Hult** | **Recommended** | Standard vehicle for short, outcomes-focused programs; flexible pass/fail; Hult-branded credential; campus and systems access without degree-program constraints. |

Each cohort is a **discrete enrollment term** (one semester). Repeat enrollees register for a new term on their existing program agreement — no second tuition charge, but a registrar enrollment record each time.

### Credit and credential

**Recommendation:** **Certificate only, zero academic credit.** Graduates receive a Hult-issued *Cohort Developer Certificate* plus the program's GitHub-verifiable credentials (winner/operator, leadership team — see [governance/credentials.md](../governance/credentials.md)).

The certificate requires:
- Completion of all six project units (Phase 1 × 3, Phase 2 × 3)
- Pass determination per [assessment/pass-fail.md](../assessment/pass-fail.md)
- Public portfolio on the cohort showcase platform

No transfer to degree programs unless Hult separately approves a pathway (out of scope for cohort 1).

## Refund policy compatibility

The program promises **quit anytime for a refund** and **unlimited free repeat enrollment until job offer**. Hult's standard refund schedules typically prorate by week. These conflict unless scoped.

### Recommended refund schedule (aligned with guarantee)

| Timing | Refund |
|--------|--------|
| Before cohort start (any time up to day before week 1) | **100%** of tuition ($10,000) |
| Week 1 (onboarding) | **100%** if student withdraws by Friday 5pm local (cohort campus timezone) |
| Weeks 2–16 | **No cash refund** — student retains unlimited repeat enrollment rights until job offer or buyout |
| Program-initiated buyout | **$5,000** cash + release from all obligations (see [business/guarantee-refunds-buyouts.md](../business/guarantee-refunds-buyouts.md)) |

**Reasoning:** Full refund through week 1 preserves the marketing promise ("quit anytime") for anyone who discovers misfit early, while preventing mid-semester cash-refund gaming after receiving curriculum value. After week 1, the student's remedy is re-enrollment, not cash — which matches the near-zero marginal cost model.

⚠️ **DECISION NEEDED (EVP + General Counsel):** Confirm Hult's continuing-ed refund policy can adopt this schedule, or whether state/regulatory rules (MA, CA, UK campuses) require a different floor.

### Tooling subscriptions

Claude Code and Cursor subscriptions are **student-paid directly to vendors**, not through Hult. Refunds on those are between the student and the vendor per vendor policy. Hult does not refund tooling costs.

## Repeat enrollment

### Mechanics

- One $10,000 tuition payment covers **all future cohort terms** until a qualifying job offer (see guarantee doc).
- Each re-enrollment: student completes a **term registration form** (no payment) → registrar creates enrollment record → student gets cohort GitHub org access.
- No cap on repeat terms. No retake fee.

### Enrollment caps and visa

| Concern | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Cohort size cap | Operational cap of **40 students per cohort** (see [operations/admissions.md](../operations/admissions.md)); not an enrollment-policy cap on repeaters. |
| Repeat enrollee priority | Returners register **after** new admits for seat allocation; if a cohort is full, returners get first waitlist priority. |
| International students (F-1) | Each repeat term may require **updated enrollment verification** for visa compliance. Program staff flags repeaters to International Student Services at registration. |
| Time-to-completion limits | **None.** The guarantee explicitly allows unlimited terms. |

⚠️ **DECISION NEEDED (EVP + International Student Services):** Confirm visa rules for non-credit continuing-ed students repeating terms across multiple semesters without additional tuition.

## Pass/fail and the job guarantee

### Assessment model

- **Pass/fail only.** No letter grades, no GPA.
- Pass = completed all six units at minimum thresholds (defined in assessment docs).
- Fail = did not meet thresholds; may re-enroll free.

This fits continuing-ed certificate conventions and avoids accreditation triggers tied to degree-program grading.

### Job guarantee vs. accreditation

The guarantee ("complete successfully → job offer") is a **commercial enrollment agreement term**, not an academic accreditation claim. It should be:

1. Documented in a **separate Program Agreement** signed at enrollment (not buried in the general catalog).
2. Scoped with explicit definitions of "qualifying job offer" and student obligations (participation minimums).
3. Reviewed by General Counsel before any marketing use of the word "guaranteed."

⚠️ **DECISION NEEDED (General Counsel):** Approve guarantee language for marketing and the Program Agreement template.
