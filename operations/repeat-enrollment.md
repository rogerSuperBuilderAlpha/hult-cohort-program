# Repeat enrollment

**Purpose:** Unlimited cohort re-enrollment on single $10k fee until qualifying job offer. Normal path, not remedial.

---

## Who re-enrolls

| Reason | Common? |
|--------|---------|
| Failed cohort | Yes |
| Passed, wants more reps | Yes |
| Platform winner, lighter load next term | Yes |
| Passed, no offer yet after 180 days | Yes (guarantee) |
| Passed, got offer | **No** — enrollment closed |

---

## Returner experience

**Redo everything.** New cohort org, new platforms, new builds, new reviews.

This is fine — each cohort's ecosystem is new. Returners are not exempt from Project 1–3 builds.

Returners **may not** copy prior cohort code into contest submissions (same plagiarism rule).

---

## Returner advantages

| Advantage | Handicapping |
|-----------|--------------|
| Know the loop | **None** — earned advantage |
| Faster reviews | Expected |
| May win operator again | Allowed |

No vote handicapping. Returners disclose "cohort N" on application.

---

## Registration

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Submit term registration form (no payment) |
| 2 | Priority: **after** new admits if cohort full |
| 3 | Waitlist: returners **first** on waitlist |
| 4 | Tooling: **$400/month** each term (student-paid) |
| 5 | ISS notification if international |

---

## Capacity

Returners **count toward cohort max (40)**. Target mix: ≤ 30% returners (12 max in cohort of 40) to preserve new-student culture.

If returner demand exceeds 30%: lottery among returners; remainder guaranteed next term.

---

## Credentials

Stack per term ([credentials.md](../governance/credentials.md)). Multiple winner/operator badges across terms = strong signal.

---

## When program declines re-enrollment

Rare. Softer than buyout:

| Ground | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Repeated conduct violations | Decline + refund not available (post week 1) |
| 3 consecutive fails with no metric improvement | Director conversation; may suggest buyout |
| Cohort full + returner cap | Waitlist only |

Program **cannot** decline solely for failing — guarantee requires unlimited enrollment.
