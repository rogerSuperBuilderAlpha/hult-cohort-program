# Hiring partners

**Purpose:** Build and operate the employer pipeline that makes the job guarantee real and generates referral fee revenue. Partners review cohort work, hire graduates, and pay ~25% of first-year base salary on successful hire.

The hiring partner pipeline must be **contractually engaged before cohort 1 week 16** (December 19, 2026). Recruiting partners starts **12 weeks before cohort 1 start** (target: June 2026).

---

## Target partner profile

### Ideal company

| Attribute | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Stage | **Series A through public** — has hiring budget and engineering org |
| Size | **50–2,000 employees** — large enough to hire regularly, small enough to move fast |
| Hiring volume | **≥ 5 software engineers in the next 12 months** |
| Tech culture | Uses GitHub for engineering; comfortable hiring from public commit history |
| Agent fluency | Building with AI-assisted development or actively exploring it |
| Geography | US, UK, EU — aligned with Hult campus locations and graduate visa paths |

### Tier 1 targets (best fit)

- Developer tools companies (Cursor ecosystem, Vercel, GitHub-adjacent startups)
- Fintech and B2B SaaS with strong eng cultures
- Hult corporate partners and alumni-founded companies
- Consulting firms with dedicated engineering practices (not body shops)

### Disqualifiers

- Staffing agencies (unless end-client direct hire with signed fee agreement)
- Companies requiring clearance the program can't support in cohort 1
- Partners unwilling to pay referral fee on hire
- Partners wanting exclusive access to all graduates (we offer no exclusivity)

---

## Partner pitch

**One sentence:** *We produce developers you can evaluate entirely on GitHub — every review, every deployment, every merged PR is public — and you pay only when you hire.*

### Pitch deck sections (4 slides)

1. **The proof problem:** Resumes lie; GitHub doesn't. Show a sample cohort showcase and a graduate's six-project trail.
2. **What graduates can do:** Review others' work, ship production apps, land open source merges, operate live platforms with real users.
3. **How hiring works:** Week-16 showcase → async portfolio review → intro requests → your interview process → hire → fee on start date.
4. **Commercial terms:** 25% of first-year base salary, 90-day clawback, 10% kickback to candidate (shows we align with them, not against them).

### Fee justification

At $200k hire → $50k fee. Partner gets: 16 weeks of observable work, peer-reviewed builds, production deployments, open source merges, and showcase access. Comparable agency fee: 20–30% with less signal.

## Partner agreement (standard terms)

### Referral fee

| Term | Value |
|------|-------|
| Fee | **25% of first-year base salary** (signing bonus excluded unless > 20% of total comp, then included) |
| Trigger | Candidate **starts employment** |
| Invoice | Net 30 from start date |
| Clawback | **100% refund of fee if employee terminates or is terminated for cause within 90 days** |
| Exclusivity | **None** — partner has no exclusive access; students may receive multiple offers |
| Non-circumvention | **12 months** — if partner hires a graduate they were introduced to, fee applies even if hire is delayed |

### Candidate access rules

- Partners receive access to the **public showcase platform** (week 8 onward as it goes live).
- Partners may request **intro calls** with specific students via placement lead (student opt-in required).
- Partners may **not** contact students directly outside the placement process until student opts in.
- Partners may run **their own technical interviews**; program does not substitute for partner hiring bar.

### Data and privacy

- Partner receives only **public portfolio data** and student-consented profile information.
- Partner agrees to **EEOC/equivalent compliance** in hiring decisions.

## Engagement model through the semester

Partners are **not** week-16-only. Early visibility improves hire quality and partner commitment.

| Phase | Week | Partner touchpoint |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| Pre-cohort | −12 to 0 | Term negotiation, showcase RSVP, partner page on future showcase |
| Phase 1 | 8 | **Showcase platform goes live** — partners get read-only access to cohort public work |
| Phase 1 | 8–16 | Optional: partners may watch repos, leave public GitHub issues/comments (encouraged) |
| Phase 2 | 9–15 | Partners see learning apps and venture projects appear on showcase |
| Phase 2 | 16 | **Hiring partner showcase** — live review event ([showcase-event.md](showcase-event.md)) |
| Post-cohort | 16–52 | Placement window: intros, interviews, offers; 180-day guarantee clock starts at pass date |

### Partner portal (minimum viable)

Hosted on the cohort showcase platform (built by students, week 7–8):

- Partner login (read-only)
- Graduate profiles with links to GitHub, apps, open source PRs
- "Request intro" button → routes to placement lead
- RSVP for week-16 event

No separate partner portal build by program staff — dogfood the cohort's Project 3.

## Interest routing

When a partner wants to engage a specific student:

```
Partner clicks "Request intro" on showcase
  → Placement lead receives ticket (24hr SLA)
  → Placement lead confirms student opt-in
  → If yes: calendar intro (partner + student + placement lead optional)
  → CRM updated: Partner X → Student Y → Intro sent → Interview → Offer
  → Offer verified against guarantee criteria
  → Referral fee invoiced on start date
  → 10% kickback to student within 30 days of fee collection
```

Student may decline any intro with no penalty. Three consecutive declines without cause triggers a placement-process conversation (not guarantee void — see guarantee doc).

Self-sourced offers: student notifies placement lead → offer verified → guarantee fulfilled → no referral fee unless partner was previously introduced (non-circumvention applies).
